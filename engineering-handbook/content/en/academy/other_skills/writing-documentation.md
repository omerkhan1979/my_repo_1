This article is designed to be an eductaional resource for anyone at Takeoff who wants to learn more about writing and documentation. In particular, this page focuses on how to become "subject matter experts" in Takeoff Engineering and Product documentation.   

## Become a Client-Facing/Zendesk Documentation Reviewer

Takeoff client-facing documentation reviewers have the ability to approve and publish articles in Zendesk. They understand our Product documentation resources, processes, and can provide guidance to other Takeoff employees who are trying to create or update documentation for our clients.  

To become a client-facing documentation subject matter expert:

1. Start by reading the article [Your Company Is Only as Good as Your Writing](https://hbr.org/2013/07/your-company-is-only-as-good-a). 
2. Complete [Google's Technical Writing One, and Technical Writing Two courses](https://developers.google.com/tech-writing/overview).
3. Complete steps 1 and 2 of the [Zendesk Guide Self Service course](https://training.zendesk.com/path/self-service).
4. Read our [Style Guide](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/styleguide/).
5. Read and make at least 1 contribution to each of the articles in the [How to contribute client-facing documentation](https://takeofftech.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TE/pages/3247505485/How+to+contribute+to+client-facing+documentation) article set in Confluence. 
6. Once you have completed the above, [submit a request to be a Zendesk documentation reviewer on the TD Kanban](https://takeofftech.atlassian.net/secure/CreateIssue.jspa?issuetype=10100&pid=10261). Include your certificate for completing the Google Tech Writing courses.

## Become an Engineering Documentation Subject-Matter Expert

Engineering Documentation Subject Matter Experts have the ability to review and approve pull requests to our Engineering Handbook. They understand our Engineering documentation resources, processes, and can provide guidance to other Takeoff employees who are trying to create or update documentation in our Engineering Handbook. 

To become an engineering handbook documentation subject matter expert:

1. Start by reading [Undervalued Skills](https://blog.pragmaticengineer.com/on-writing-well/) and our own [Handbook-first approach](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/handbook_first/) article (inspired by [Gitlab](https://about.gitlab.com/company/culture/all-remote/handbook-first-documentation/)).
2. Complete [Google's Technical Writing One, and Technical Writing Two courses](https://developers.google.com/tech-writing/overview). 
3. Read our [Style Guide](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/styleguide/).
4. Learn [markdown](https://docs.github.com/en/github/writing-on-github/getting-started-with-writing-and-formatting-on-github/basic-writing-and-formatting-syntax).
5. Complete [GitHub first day](https://lab.github.com/githubtraining/first-day-on-github) and [GitHub first week](https://lab.github.com/githubtraining/first-week-on-github) if you haven't already.
6. Install Hugo and set up an Integrated Development Environment (IDE) - borrow from the theme's [getting started guide](https://www.docsy.dev/docs/getting-started/).
   1. Understand shortcodes [Docsy shortcodes](https://www.docsy.dev/docs/adding-content/shortcodes/) and [Hugo shortcodes](https://gohugo.io/content-management/shortcodes/).
 
7. Read and understand the following articles: 
   1. [Handbook Infrastructure](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/handbook_infra/)  
   3. [Markdown Reference](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/markdown_ref/)
   4. [Site Reference](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/site_ref/)
   5. [Multimedia Reference](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/site_ref/)
   6. [How to Contribute](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/contributing/)

8. Complete at least one of the following exercises:
   1. Make a [contribution to the Handbook](https://engineering-handbook.takeofftech.org/docs/handbook/contributing/) (or Convert something from Confluence). 
   2. Add [at least three items to our backlog](https://takeofftech.atlassian.net/jira/software/c/projects/EH/boards/281/backlog?selectedIssue=EH-24&issueLimit=100&view=detail). 
   3. Create your own Hugo-based personal Resume site.

9.  Once you have completed the above, [submit a request to be a Zendesk documentation reviewer on the TD Kanban](https://takeofftech.atlassian.net/secure/CreateIssue.jspa?issuetype=10100&pid=10261). Include your certificate for completing the Google Tech Writing courses and a link to your completed exercise.
